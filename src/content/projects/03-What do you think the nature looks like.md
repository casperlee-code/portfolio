專案概述

"What do you think the nature looks like?" This question emerged from our observations along Taiwan's east coast, spanning Yilan and Hualian. In this relatively fragile yet pristine region, we sought the connection between the city and the ocean. Through film and curation, we guide the audience to reflect on the relationship between civilization and nature.

Program: AA Visiting School (Urbanity from the ocean)

Site Location: Yilan & Hualian, Taiwan

Keyword: Field Research / Film Documentary / Curation

Curatorial Statement

Have you ever thought about what nature looks like? The greenness of the mountains, the blueness of the sea, and the vitality of plants and animals are nature, but what about humans?

"The Hunter's Oath of the Bunun" is a story passed down from generation to generation by the Bunun people to educate the younger generation on hunting norms and how to respect nature. Hunters and prey make us think about whether humans' constant demands on nature are for survival or just greed.

And is nature really just like what humans know? Is it just resources that are constantly developed and fisheries that are constantly caught?

Through these questions, we hope to inspire people to think that nature is not just what it looks like in front of them. The stone that appears at the beginning and end of the film is not defined because it can be anything, just like humans have different views on the nature.

創作與策展內容

Film Documentary
Discovering nature's vulnerability and embrace through fieldwork, our lens captures the profound bond between humanity and the natural world.

Stone (Metaphor)
A stone can embody everything, yet quietly fade into dust. We view the coastal pebbles as a shared metaphor, reflecting both nature and humanity.

Weaving the Perspectives
Rather than dwelling on how humanity tears nature apart, this exhibition weaves together our perspectives and actions, inviting quiet reflection within the interactive space.

Curation
Fusing imagery, interaction, and introspection, the exhibition immerses viewers in our 14-day journey of observation. This is not merely an ecological documentary or a news report, but a heartfelt reflection on the city, the ocean, and nature itself.
