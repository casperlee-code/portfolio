School Project — 2024

Conflicts in the Harmony

專案概述

“Public restrooms and parks represent the most liberated corners of this city. People enter freely to rest, chat, and unwind before moving on. I have always felt that Taiwan, teeming with vitality, may not be ‘beautiful’ in the conventional sense, but it is undoubtedly interesting — all compressed into the fabric of our daily existence.”

Site Location: Xitun District, Taichung

Keyword: The contradictions of Taiwan’s urban streetscape

Tools: Rhino, AutoCAD, Photoshop, Illustrator

設計理念：Common city scene for Taiwan

Public toilets and parks are the freest corners of a city. People freely enter parks to rest, chat, and relax, and then leave.

For those of us who have lived in Taiwan for a long time, we are likely familiar, accustomed, or even numb to the things before our eyes. On this densely populated land, factories, parks, commercial areas, residential zones, and farmlands all converge. Various textures of urban elements gather here. These different elements collide, combine, and compromise, ultimately forming a harmonious and stable state.

The Site

The site, in the middle of the small park, represents a collage of heterogeneous elements — religious spaces (Temple), public amenities (Public toilet), and play areas. This juxtaposition mirrors the complex fabric of Taiwanese cities. Here, contradictory spatial narratives and visual lines are reconciled, integrating harmoniously into the rhythm of everyday existence.

空間與造型策略

People live in the city, going to work, coming home, resting, relaxing, and chatting. Parks have become an essential “third space” in their daily lives. Children play, adults relax, the elderly gather, passersby use the restroom, and worshippers come to pray. This place seems to be a microcosm of the city and society, densely packed with functions.

After closely observing the textures of the site, curves, blocks, circles, and lines converge, collide, intersect, and differ here, existing and transforming.

Guiding people to the harmony: The place that children can run, parents can rest.

Building massing (toilet/office): The children toilet → equipment / disabled toilet → road office → temple.

Various and conflicting elements: Creating the collision of different behaviors (ex: sitting, washing…).

Curved terrain: Separating different areas / creating the route.
